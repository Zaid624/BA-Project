# LinkedIn/Xing Post Draft

I turned a Superstore-style retail dataset into a Business Analyst portfolio project focused on sales, discounting, and margin performance.

The main finding: discounting above 20% is where profitability breaks down. In the cleaned dataset of 9,977 transaction lines, no-discount sales generated a 29.51% margin, while the 60%+ discount band lost $70.6K at a -122.63% margin.

The project includes stakeholder questions, a data dictionary, SQL/Python analysis outputs, a Streamlit dashboard, Power BI-ready CSV exports, data-quality tests, and executive summaries in English and German.

I also documented the limitations clearly: no order dates, customer IDs, product names, or cost-of-goods fields. That matters, because good analysis should show both what the data says and what it cannot yet prove.
