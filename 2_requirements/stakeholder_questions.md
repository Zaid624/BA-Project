# Stakeholder Questions

## Profitability

1. Which sub-categories generate the highest total profit, and should they receive more sales focus?
2. Which sub-categories lose money or produce weak margins despite generating sales?
3. Which product categories contribute the most sales, profit, and margin?
4. What is the overall sales, profit, margin, discount, unit volume, and average order value baseline?
5. At which discount levels does the business move from profitable to loss-making?

## Regional Performance

1. Which regions generate the highest sales and profit, and how do their margins compare?
2. Which states are the strongest profit contributors?
3. Which states are reducing profit and may need pricing, product mix, or sales-policy review?
4. Which cities generate the highest sales, and are those sales also profitable?
5. Which sub-categories drive sales in each region?

## Customer Behavior

1. Which customer segments generate the most sales and profit?
2. How does average order value differ by region and customer segment?
3. Which sub-categories are bought in the highest quantity?
4. Are high-revenue orders also profitable after discounting?

## Operations & Logistics

1. Which shipping modes are most frequently used by category?
2. Which shipping modes carry the highest sales volume by category?
3. Are there category-level fulfillment patterns that should be considered when prioritizing profitable growth?

## Known Requirement Constraints

The available dataset does not include order dates, order IDs, product names, customer IDs, cost-of-goods values, or campaign history. As a result, this project can assess margin, discount, regional, category, segment, and shipping patterns, but it cannot support true time-series analysis, customer lifetime value, product-level ranking, campaign attribution, or order-level duplicate checks.
