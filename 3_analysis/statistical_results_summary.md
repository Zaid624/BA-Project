# Statistical Significance — Plain Language Summary

The question: *Are the differences we see (between regions, categories, and discount bands) real, or could they be random noise?*

## 1. Regions

**Is the margin difference between regions statistically significant?** Yes — but with an important caveat.

Central is genuinely worse than the other three regions (medium-sized effect). However, the difference between East and South is **not statistically significant** — they perform similarly. West is slightly ahead of East and South, but the effect is negligible.

**Bottom line:** The region differences that matter are "Central vs everyone else." The other regions are more alike than different.

## 2. Categories

**Is the margin difference between categories significant?** Technically yes, but category explains less than 1% of the variation.

Furniture has a lower margin than Office Supplies and Technology — but the difference is small. Office Supplies and Technology are not significantly different from each other.

**Bottom line:** Category matters less than you might think. The real story is within categories (which products, which discounts), not between them.

## 3. Discount Bands

**Is the margin difference between discount bands significant?** Overwhelmingly yes.

Discount band explains **75% of all variation in margin** — the highest by far. Every discount band is significantly different from every other, and the effect sizes are large.

**Bottom line:** This confirms the headline finding with statistical rigor. Discount level is the single most important factor driving margin.

---

### Why this matters

Without statistical tests, you might look at the numbers and say "West has 14.94% margin and Central has 7.92% — that's a big difference!" The tests tell you that:
- Yes, Central is genuinely worse ✅
- But East vs South? They're basically the same ❌
- And discount level is 10x more important than region ✅
